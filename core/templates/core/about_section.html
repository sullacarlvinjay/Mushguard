About MushGuard

Empowering safe mushroom identification through AI and community science

Our Mission

MushGuard aims to build a digital biodiversity map of mushrooms found within Biliran Province. Through the power of artificial intelligence and community participation, we help users identify whether mushrooms are edible or poisonous, classify their species, and map where they were found.

This initiative not only promotes public safety but also contributes to scientific research and environmental awareness.

How It Works

1️⃣
Scan a Mushroom

Use your phone's camera or upload an image to identify a mushroom.

2️⃣
AI Identification

Our AI model checks the image using the mushroom species dataset.

3️⃣
Edibility Check

If the species is recognized, the system determines whether it's edible or poisonous.

4️⃣
New Discoveries

If the image confidence is below 75%, the system flags it as a new or unknown species.

5️⃣
Mapping

Users can then tag the location where they found the mushroom, contributing to our growing map of fungal biodiversity.

Mapping for Research

Every uploaded image helps build a living map of mushrooms in Biliran Province. This map shows the distribution of different species, tracks new discoveries, and can be used by researchers, environmentalists, and local communities to monitor changes in the ecosystem.

Powered by AI

  • Mushroom Edibility Model – Determines if a mushroom is edible or poisonous.
  • Species Classification Model – Identifies the most likely species based on image analysis.

Both models are built using TensorFlow Lite and integrated into the website for real-time predictions.

Community Collaboration

MushGuard is not just an identification tool — it's a community platform. Users who upload new or unknown mushrooms contribution, helping improve the AI model's accuracy over time.

Verified submissions are reviewed by administrators before being added to the official database.

Why It Matters

  • Promotes environmental awareness
  • Encourages citizen science participation
  • Enhances public safety against poisonous mushrooms
  • Supports biodiversity monitoring and conservation
